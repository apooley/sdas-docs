## Purpose and position in the pipeline

SDAS CRM Connector bridges SDAS events from WordPress into your downstream CRM or DMS by listening to SDAS core actions and queuing asynchronous sync jobs.

Within the SDAS lead pipeline, the connector sits after lead and vehicle events are produced by SDAS Core and related plugins, and before data leaves WordPress for your external CRM. It does not collect leads itself; instead, it reacts to SDAS events such as leads being submitted, vehicle descriptions being generated, and vehicles being marked as sold.

At runtime, WordPress loads the plugin from:

- Bootstrap file: `wp-content/plugins/sdas-crm-connector/sdas-crm-connector.php`
- Main plugin class and hook registration: `wp-content/plugins/sdas-crm-connector/includes/Plugin.php`
- Queue management and Action Scheduler bindings: `wp-content/plugins/sdas-crm-connector/includes/Queue/QueueManager.php`

<Callout kind="info">

SDAS CRM Connector synchronizes data asynchronously using Action Scheduler jobs. SDAS requests that trigger these events should complete quickly; sync retries and backoff happen in the background.

</Callout>

## Hook registration and lifecycle

`Plugin::init_hooks` wires the connector into SDAS by subscribing to three SDAS actions and forwarding payloads into Action Scheduler jobs.

### Incoming SDAS actions

The connector listens to three high-level SDAS events:

```php
add_action( 'sdas_lead_submitted', [$this, 'queue_lead_sync'], 10, 2 );
add_action( 'sdas_vehicle_description_generated', [$this, 'queue_vehicle_sync_after_description'], 10, 2 );
add_action( 'sdas_vehicle_sold', [$this, 'queue_sale_sync'], 10, 2 );
```

Each action follows the same pattern:

- The SDAS core or related plugins fire the action with structured arguments.
- The CRM Connector callback (`queue_*` methods) receives those arguments.
- The callback defers work by scheduling an internal queue action in `QueueManager`.

<Callout kind="info">

The three SDAS actions are integration points you can observe from other plugins, but the CRM Connector remains the owner of queuing CRM sync jobs. Avoid changing argument signatures or short-circuiting these hooks.

</Callout>

### `sdas_lead_submitted`

`sdas_lead_submitted` is fired when a new lead is captured in SDAS, typically from an SDAS form submission on the front end.

At a high level:

- **Producer:** SDAS Forms Extension and related lead capture flows.
- **Timing:** Immediately after the lead is validated and persisted to the SDAS data model.
- **Intent:** Announce that a lead is ready to be synchronized to external systems.

The CRM Connector handler `queue_lead_sync` receives two arguments (commonly a lead identifier and a data array) and schedules a `sdas_crm_sync_lead` job in Action Scheduler. The sync job encapsulates any mapping, transformation, and API calls required to push that lead into the target CRM.

### `sdas_vehicle_description_generated`

`sdas_vehicle_description_generated` is fired when SDAS finishes generating or updating a vehicle description, for example after an AI description job or a content update.

Conceptually:

- **Producer:** SDAS Inventory or related vehicle content tooling.
- **Timing:** After a vehicle record and its description fields have been successfully updated.
- **Intent:** Indicate that the vehicle metadata, including description, is now in a state suitable for CRM synchronization.

The CRM Connector handler `queue_vehicle_sync_after_description` consumes the event payload and schedules a `sdas_crm_sync_vehicle` job. That job is responsible for syncing the latest vehicle details and description to the CRM or DMS so that sales teams see current content.

### `sdas_vehicle_sold`

`sdas_vehicle_sold` is fired when a vehicle is marked as sold within SDAS inventory management.

In practice:

- **Producer:** SDAS Inventory or any custom sales workflow that updates vehicle status.
- **Timing:** When the status change to "sold" has been persisted in WordPress.
- **Intent:** Propagate the sale event, including buyer and pricing context when available, to the CRM.

The CRM Connector handler `queue_sale_sync` uses the sale context to schedule a `sdas_crm_sync_sale` job, which updates the external CRM with sale details, status changes, and any associated lead records.

## Internal queued actions

The CRM Connector uses `QueueManager` to create Action Scheduler jobs for each type of sync. These jobs decouple SDAS request handling from external CRM latency and failures.

`QueueManager` schedules three internal queue actions:

- `sdas_crm_sync_lead`
- `sdas_crm_sync_vehicle`
- `sdas_crm_sync_sale`

### `sdas_crm_sync_lead` (lead sync job)

`sdas_crm_sync_lead` represents a unit of work to push a single SDAS lead into the external CRM.

Typical responsibilities of the job include:

- Loading the SDAS lead record based on identifiers passed from `sdas_lead_submitted`.
- Mapping SDAS lead fields to the CRM lead or contact schema.
- Executing network calls to the CRM API to create or update the corresponding record.
- Handling transient failures with retries, backoff, or error logging.

<Callout kind="info">

Because lead sync runs as a background job, short network outages or CRM rate limits usually do not affect the user who submitted the form. Monitor the Action Scheduler queue and the connector logs to detect persistent failures.

</Callout>

### `sdas_crm_sync_vehicle` (vehicle sync job)

`sdas_crm_sync_vehicle` encapsulates a vehicle-level sync operation.

The job typically:

- Loads a vehicle record after `sdas_vehicle_description_generated`.
- Extracts key inventory attributes such as VIN, stock number, pricing, photos, and descriptions.
- Aligns those attributes to the CRM or DMS vehicle representation.
- Updates or creates corresponding vehicle entries in the external system.

When vehicle descriptions are generated or improved, `sdas_crm_sync_vehicle` ensures those updates flow through to CRM tools used by sales and marketing.

### `sdas_crm_sync_sale` (sale sync job)

`sdas_crm_sync_sale` carries out synchronization of sale events.

Within the job:

- The job retrieves the vehicle and sale context associated with `sdas_vehicle_sold`.
- Leads or contacts related to the sale may be cross-referenced to update their lifecycle stage in the CRM.
- Sale data such as sale date, final price, gross, salesperson, and store can be transmitted to the CRM (subject to implementation).

This job keeps the CRM aligned with SDAS on inventory disposition and deal outcomes.

## Listening to SDAS hooks from another plugin

You can attach your own listeners to SDAS actions for auditing, analytics, or side effects without interfering with the CRM Connector. For example, a custom plugin might log every submitted lead to an internal audit log.

Use standard WordPress `add_action` calls with a later priority so that the CRM Connector can still enqueue its jobs as expected.

<CodeGroup tabs="PHP">

```php
<?php
/**
 * Plugin Name: SDAS Lead Audit Logger
 * Description: Logs SDAS leads for internal auditing without affecting CRM Connector behavior.
 */

add_action(
    'sdas_lead_submitted',
    'sdas_lead_audit_logger_handle_lead',
    20,
    2
);

/**
 * Audit handler for SDAS lead submissions.
 *
 * @param int   $lead_id   SDAS lead identifier.
 * @param array $lead_data Associative array of lead fields from SDAS.
 */
function sdas_lead_audit_logger_handle_lead( $lead_id, $lead_data ) {
    // Example: write a minimal audit entry to a custom log file.
    $message = sprintf(
        '[%s] Lead submitted: ID=%d, Email=%s, Source=%s',
        gmdate( 'Y-m-d H:i:s' ),
        $lead_id,
        isset( $lead_data['email'] ) ? $lead_data['email'] : '(unknown)',
        isset( $lead_data['source'] ) ? $lead_data['source'] : '(unknown)'
    );

    error_log( $message, 3, WP_CONTENT_DIR . '/logs/sdas-lead-audit.log' );

    // Keep work here non-blocking; do not make slow network calls.
}
```

</CodeGroup>

<Callout kind="info">

Keep custom `sdas_lead_submitted` listeners fast and non-blocking. If you need to perform slow operations or external API calls, schedule your own Action Scheduler job instead of doing work inline, so that SDAS form submissions stay responsive.

</Callout>
