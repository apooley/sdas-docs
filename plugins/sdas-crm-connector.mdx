## Overview

The SDAS CRM Connector is a powerful integration tool that bridges the gap between your WordPress-based lead capture and your external CRM or DMS. It automatically synchronizes leads, vehicle data, and sales events to ensure your sales team has the most up-to-date information in their primary CRM tool.

## Microsoft Dynamics 365 Integration

The connector is specifically optimized for **Microsoft Dynamics 365**, providing a deep, automated integration that handles complex data mapping and validation.

### Authentication and Setup

The connector uses secure **OAuth2** authentication to connect to your Dynamics 365 organization.

1.  **Connect**: Navigate to `SDAS -> CRM Settings` and click the "Connect to Dynamics" button.
2.  **Authorize**: You will be redirected to the Microsoft Online login page to authorize the SDAS application.
3.  **Configure**: Once connected, you can define the **Destination Table** (e.g., Leads, Contacts) where new leads should be created.

### Intelligent Field Mapping

The system includes an **Intelligent Mapping** engine that automatically aligns your WordPress form fields with your Dynamics schema.

-   **Automatic Detection**: The connector analyzes your Dynamics entity schema and automatically maps fields based on their type, label, and content.
-   **Complex Field Handling**: Name and address fields are intelligently split into their respective Dynamics subfields (e.g., First Name, Last Name, Street, City).
-   **Data Transformation**: Values are automatically transformed to match Dynamics requirements (e.g., "Yes/No" becomes boolean, dates are converted to ISO format).
-   **Attribution Data**: Tracking identifiers like GCLID and other attribution data are automatically flattened and mapped to your Dynamics fields.

### Reviewing Mappings

You can review and audit the current field mappings on the **Field Mapping Details** screen (`SDAS -> Field Mapping`):

-   **Mapping Statistics**: See a summary of how many fields are mapped across different Dynamics entities (Contact, Lead, Credit Application, Attribution Data).
-   **Detailed Audit**: View a comprehensive table of all active mappings, including the WPForms Field ID, the target Dynamics Field, the data type, and any specialized processors being used.

## The Lead Synchronization Flow

The connector operates asynchronously to ensure that form submissions remain fast and reliable for your visitors.

1.  **Lead Capture**: A visitor submits a form on your site (e.g., a "Get a Quote" or "Credit Application" form).
2.  **Validation & Persistence**: SDAS Core validates the lead and saves it to the WordPress database.
3.  **Event Trigger**: The `sdas_lead_submitted` action is fired, signaling that a new lead is ready for sync.
4.  **Queueing**: The CRM Connector receives the event and adds a "Sync Lead" job to the **Action Scheduler** queue.
5.  **Background Sync**: The system processes the sync job in the background, performing the necessary data transformations and calling the Dynamics API to create the record.
6.  **Error Handling**: If the sync fails (e.g., due to a network issue), the system automatically retries the job with an exponential backoff strategy.

## Vehicle and Sale Synchronization

In addition to leads, the connector can also synchronize inventory-related events:

-   **Vehicle Sync**: When a vehicle's description is generated or updated (e.g., via the AI Description Generator), the connector can sync the latest details to your CRM.
-   **Sale Sync**: When a vehicle is marked as "Sold" in the SDAS Inventory, the connector can propagate this status change and any associated sale data to the CRM.

## Monitoring and Diagnostics

-   **Sync Status**: Monitor the status of all background sync jobs in the **Action Scheduler** (`Tools -> Scheduled Actions`).
-   **Diagnostics**: Use the built-in diagnostic tools to test your CRM connection and verify that the Dynamics API is reachable and responding correctly.
-   **Logging**: Detailed logs are available to help troubleshoot mapping issues or API errors.
